All About Plums

How do plums grow?

Plums prefer slightly acid soil (pH 6.0-6.8) and thrive in lightweight loamy, well-draining soil. Plum trees grow and produce the best fruit in well-drained but moist fertile soil that is rich in organic matter, but tolerate a wide range of soils as long as water and nutrients are not limiting and soil pH is adequate.

What season do plums grow in?

The fruit is in season in the Philippines, from mid-May to mid-June. Plums usually require a tepid or temperate climate for its growth.

How Long Do Plums Last?

Plums develop for 3 to 4 months from blossoms, and ripen from hard and green to fully colored and soft on the tree. Plums will only be ripe on the tree for about 2 weeks and drop as they become over-ripe. There may be a mix of green-ish unripe fruit and fully ripe fruit on the tree at one time.

How to Know if it's Spoiled?

Fresh plums have a gorgeous dark purple color. Plums that are no longer fresh might become discolored or misshapen. Also, if your plums' skin has become wrinkly, it is an indication that your plums aren't fresh anymore. Plums that have spots, mold, or growths on them should never be eaten.
